About

Polaris is an open-source website and framework for universities and organizations to develop campus maps that crowdsource data on accessible routes and potential obstacles.

History

The idea for this project came from the difficulties experienced by one of the project creators (a wheelchair user) and others with limited mobility while navigating their large university campus.

Its initial implementation was done by members of Team Polaris of UNC-CH (alphabetically listed here by last name):

  • Elaine Dong
  • Christine Mendoza
  • Joe Spencer
  • Robert Susewell
  • Mia Ziade

Its name, Polaris, comes from the North Star (the main star used for navigation)!
